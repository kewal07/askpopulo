Valentine’s Day : Roses, Hugs, Kisses, Love and Rs 22000 Crores

Created by: Kewal Krishna 8th Feb, 2016 Read Time: 3 Mins

Is it about that crimson delicate rose which you picked aside from a bunch of thrones or is it about that promise you made to be there forever ? Is it about that warm reassuring hug while you sniff the locks around the neck or is it about that stolen kiss which you forever cherish ? Is this Valentines Day going to be about your loved one and you ? Well, my dear friend, i am afraid it is not. In fact, as Meja says, It's all 'bout the money ; It’s all 'bout the dum dum…

Let us have a look at The Numbers

Rs 22000 Crores ! Yes, you read it right. That was the estimated amount of money spent on this festival of Love last year, or should we say the Hallmark festival of Love. And, if we go by the growth rate of 40% , which was the last year trend, then surely this year may see an excess of Rs 25000 Crores. Indians splurge this money on cards, flowers, chocolates, soft toys, jewellery and many more, revealed an ASSOCHAM report .

India, is not alone in spending big bucks on Valentine’s day. The estimated spent in the US is around USD 18.9 Billion, way more than any other nation. To put that number into perspective, it is almost greater than 3.5% of the GDP of 175 countries of the world. Yes, just one week of spend is much more, almost seven times more than the net profits of Infosys over a quarter. Another interesting point to note is that rural spend is also increasing on this occasion by almost 22%, a couple of points more than the urban spend.

Retailers are offering attractive prices and discounts and targeting people working in IT, BPO-ITeS, retail, hospitality industry, big corporates and also students of various colleges, schools and institutions. As per the same ASSOCHAM report, almost 52% of shoppers surveyed say that they prefer to shop onlinebecause of better deals. Half of them and almost 18% of tablet owners said that the purchase is done on mobile devices itself and it should continue to grow further in the coming years.

A lot of people believe that there is no reason to have a special day to celebrate romantic relationships. Every day should be a Valentine's Day if your relationship is healthy. The only people who profit from Valentine's Day are flower sellers and chocolate manufacturers.

Having a special holiday set aside is just another example of rampant consumerism. It leads to disappointment and stress if one partner forgets, and overspending in order to convince the other partner that the relationship is important.

Inspite of all this, Valentine's day remains one of the most celebrated occassions today. Infact, this has become one of the three most important shopping festivals including Diwali and Raksha Bandhan.

Valentine's day is a day to celebrate the spirit of love and it should be celebrated with your loved ones. A quote from Reeva Steenkamp seems appropriate here.

I've realized that although Valentine's Day can be a cheesy money-making stint to most people, it's a day of expressing love across the world. It doesn't have to only be between lovers, but by telling a friend that you care, or even an old person that they are still appreciated.
